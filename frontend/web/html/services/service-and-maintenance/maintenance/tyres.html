    HOW TO CHECK YOUR TYRES

    and what to do if they need changing

    The tyres form part of the 'Triangle of Safety' along with the brakes and shock absorbers.

    They are the single point of contact between your vehicle and the road.

    It is therefore important to take care of them.

    *155/70R13 Fuel efficiency ‘E’, Wet Grip ‘C’, and external road noise ‘2’ (70dB)

    Renault Advice

    Some signs may indicate wear on your tyres:

    • Insufficient tyre pressure
    • Worn or distorted tread
    • Vibrations in the steering wheel or unusual noises in the cabin
    • A tendency for the vehicle to pull to one side instead of rolling straight
    • A deterioration of handling on wet roads
    If you notice any of these signs, it is important to check your tyres, your safety and that of your passengers depend on it. Check your tyres at your nearest Renault Dealer. Our experts are here to help you.

     

    In order to prolong the life of your tyres, we recommend that you:

    • Check pressure monthly and before any long journey
    • Monitor the state of wear of your tyres regularly
    • Change your tyres in pairs. Raise new tyres at the rear
    • Get the balancing checked and set correctly
    Under inflation of tyres can lead to a tyre blowout. You will find the ideal pressure inside your door. The pressure must be taken cold (when you have driven less than 2 miles).
     
    In the winter months, winter tyres are more efficient when the temperature is below 7(degrees)C. Contact your nearest Renault Dealer.
