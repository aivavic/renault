    Charging

    No fuss, clean and easy, for your everyday life.

    Charging Network

    A charging network is a network of publicly accessible charge points of various amounts of power. This system has been created through the combination of work by network providers, car manufacturers and the government.

    In total there are over 6500 charge points spread across the UK that are accessible to the public through the use of R.F.I.D cards and smart phone applications.

    The main manufacturers of these are:

    Ecotricity

    Ecotricity has its own charging network called ‘the Ecotricity Electric Highway’. This is a growing network of over 500 rapid chargers that are free to use. The points are located along motorways and aim to connect EV drivers across the UK.

    • Register for your free ecotricity card

    Pod Point

    The Pod Point network is one of the UK’s earliest EV charging networks. A £12.50 annual registration will give you access to about 1200 charging points spread over the UK. It works with a number of different providers to offer a range of charging options from 3kW to 43kW. Pod Point has recently announced that they will be switching to a Pay as you go scheme by 2015.

    • Register for your Pod Point card

    Polar

    Charge Master Network (powered by polar energy) is one of the UK’s largest charging networks and has about 4000 charging points in the UK. There are several membership options to keep you connected. There is the option of a £20 annual subscription or simply using the pay as you go. To register for your Polar membership card: please e-mail here or telephone 01582 400 331 during office hours to obtain an application form.

    CHARGING YOUR KANGOO

    With the Kangoo Van Z.E., driving is a very, very different affair. First, there’s the silence.

    The silence of a 100% electric motor, which gives instant pulling power and acceleration. Then, there’s simplicity. And because everything we’ve designed is to make electric the trip of tomorrow, there are two easy ways to charge your vehicle: the Single Wall-Box (home charging solution) and charging solutions at your workplace, or in public areas like supermarket car parks.
