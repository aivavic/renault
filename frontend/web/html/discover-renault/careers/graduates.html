    Graduate programmes

    Develop your passion for business

    In the UK, Renault has successfully operated graduate programmes for a number of years to identify and develop leadership talent. Working within a highly competitive commercial industry we recognise the importance of creating the right environment for graduates to develop core skills and knowledge in the early years of their career. That is why all our programmes involve a comprehensive induction and personal development plans as well as hands on work experience in challenging business roles. Ideal for graduates interested in developing a career within retail or sales management, marketing or finance. Our bespoke programmes can vary in length from eighteen month to three year rotational programmes and offer graduates the opportunity to experience working in several disciplines within our UK operation.

    Designed to fast track graduates into management, as a Renault graduate you can expect to:

    • Experience a number of different business disciplines

    • Gain a detailed understanding of our operation

    • Cultivate an extensive network of contacts

    • Develop the skills required for your first management role

    It doesn’t stop there though, our training team are committed to supporting and developing you throughout your career.

     

    Entrant Requirements:

    To succeed on a Renault Graduate Programme you need a passion for business and to be able to adapt quickly and make an impression in placements across our key business areas. You don’t need to be a petrol head but we are looking for people who share our brand vision and core values.  You need to enjoy getting involved, be open minded to ideas and opinions and be able to build trusting work relationships.

    • Minimum Grade B (Maths and English) at GCSE Levels

    • Attained a 2.1 University Degree – any discipline or final year students require 300 Min UCAS Points and must be on track for a 2.1 degree

    •Experience of working in an office/sales environment is essential, ideally an industrial placement

    •French Language skills are an advantage

    FAQ's

    How many graduates do you take on each year?

    It varies each year, depending on business need, this year we are taking on five graduates.

    What is the package?

    Your starting salary will be £27,000 p.a., 24 days paid holiday plus a car (e.g Clio), private medical insurance, with the opportunity to join a pension scheme. 

    Can nationals from outside the EU apply to the Graduate Development Programme?

    If you are not an EEA or Swiss national you will need a work permit in order to work in the UK. If you possess a valid work permit or you will be able to attain one before our October start date please go ahead and apply.

    Please note as this is a training programme Renault would not offer sponsorship at this time.

    Am I too old to apply to the Graduate Development Programme?

    Absolutely not! We have never operated an age restriction on our Graduate Development Programme and welcome applications regardless of your age.

    Do you accept paper applications?

    Paper applications will always be read, but we only accept on-line applications so we can accurately track and record your details. If you have any problems applying this way please email us here.

    What is the structure of the Graduate Development Programme?

    The structure will depend on the graduate programme you are assigned to. At telephone interview our team of interviewers will discuss your motivations, career aspirations and skills. They will use this information to assign your application to the most appropriate  scheme. Schemes vary in length from 18 months to three years. All schemes involve a mixture of core skills training and challenging work placements from day one.

    What sort of training is offered?

    During your training period you will be immersed in the company, its products and services and have access to both formal and on the job training to ensure you are well equipped for your career ahead.

    Where will I be based?

    Depending upon where you start our programme, you could be based at Renault UK's Head Office in Maple Cross, RCI Financial Services Headquarters in Watford or any of the Renault Retail Group sites.

    What type of role will I move into after my training has finished?

    Once you have completed your scheme, we will place you in your first management role which will allow you to utilise the experience you have gained during your training programme. placements from day one.

    Can I work abroad?

    Although based in the UK, International mobility is a possibility but only for high achievers who wish to work abroad. It is unlikely to take place during your training period.

    Will I get to meet other graduates on the Programme?

    Yes, there will be plenty of opportunity to network with our current graduates before you join and you’ll also spend a fair amount of time getting to know the other new starters – both formally and socially – during the induction period.

    I have previously submitted an application and was not successful, can I re-apply?

    Yes, we will consider each application on its own merit, even if you have been unsuccessful in the past.

    Recruitment calendar

    We'll review your application as soon as we receive it so there's no hanging around! If we think we're a good match for each other, we'll be calling you to discuss your application in detail and to arrange the next steps. Successful candidates will be invited to an assessment centre in June, followed by job offers by the end of July.
